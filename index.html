Who are we?

We are the Pitt Robotics and Automation Society (RAS), a student-run club that aims to provide its members with hands-on experience in robotics. Through our club, motivated individuals are given the tools and resources to develop cutting-edge autonomous robots.

Our club was founded in 2013 and since then weâ€™ve been able to work on many robots from maze navigating robots to autonomous drones, and now autonomous racecars! We are currently working on a 1:10th scale RC car for F1Tenth as well as full-sized racecars for the Indy Autonomous Challenge and Roborace. Our club is always looking for new members and would love to have you join us in developing cutting-edge technology for the future of robotics.

Autonomous Challenge

We're racing at CES 2022 in Vegas!

In an exciting sequel to the Indy Autonomous Challenge, the MIT-PITT-RW team will be participating in the world's first multi-agent, fully autononmous race at the Las Vegas Motor Speeday in January 2022. Buckle up, it's going to be a big one.

Roborace

You can count on us being there.

RoboRace is an exciting multi-agent autonomous race featuring a new vehicle (DevBot 2.0) and perhaps the biggest twist in racing yet - integration with the metaverse. Be sure to watch our team help create and compete in Motorsports 2.0.

F1Tenth

(Since less is more, right?)

What is F1Tenth?

The goal of F1Tenth is build an 1/10th scale F1 car capable of racing autonmously around a track and avoid static obstacles.

How can I join?

Just message us! No experience is necessary to join. Come help us build our racing platform, implement sophisted control, interface with cameras and LiDAR, and integrate it all together.
